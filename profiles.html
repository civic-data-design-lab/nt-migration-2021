What's driving this intention is largely economics, as 87% of households report being satisfied where they live, but desire to migrate for better economic conditions.

Migration Leads to Family Separation

13% of migrants are between 0 and 17 years of age. Many migrants leave their children with family and relatives because they don't want them to make the dangerous trip.

55% are in their Prime Working Age

Migrants ranging from age 18 to 34 represented 55% of the respondents. This is a significant loss of productive workforce in home countries.

Migration with help of a Smuggler is the Most Expensive and Most Common.

Estimates show that it cost migrants close to $1.7 Billion to travel using a smuggler. Migrants use smugglers because they protect them on the trip, but often also use narcotraffic routes and bribes to get the migrants through government check-points.
