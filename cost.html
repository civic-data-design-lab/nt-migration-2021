Irregular Migration Cost are High in Risk and Finances

Roughly 90% of migrants to the US from El Salvador, Guatemala, and Honduras use Irregular Pathways to Migrate. These pathways are extremely dangerous as only 57% of migrants make it, with huge personal cost in debt and well-being.

Migration with help of a Smuggler is the Most Expensive and Most Common.

Estimates show that it cost migrants close to $1.7 Billion to travel using a smuggler. Migrants use smugglers because they protect them on the trip, but often also use narcotraffic routes and bribes to get the migrants through government check-points.

Migration on Your Own (often using Caravans) Costs Less

Migrants who choose to migrate on their own using Caravans (mostly from Honduras) spend less time to migrate as traveling in a group protects them from smugglers who would try to extort money from them.

Regular Migration Pathways Reduces Costs

1 in 5 (19%) of migrants use regular migration pathways, including tourists, student, and farm-worker visas, which reduce cost for the migrants, but also help to reduce enforcement costs.

Migrants Have Significant Debt

Migrants fund their journey using a diverse set of funding raising efforts, much of which comes from family and friends, but also some take out loans from the bank. This risk is taken knowing the high likelihood of being apprehended at the border.

Expand Legal Pathways for Central Americans Interested in Migrating to the United States

Coordinated efforts to increase access to temporary employment visas, for example, could help meet the overwhelming demand for employment opportunities abroad. Shifting even a fraction of irregular migration to regular channels would decrease the estimated USD 1.7 billion that Central Americans spend annually on irregular migration with a smuggler and instead increase state revenues—for instance, through reasonable application fees—which can then be invested in initiatives to address other drivers of irregular migration.
